RESPONSE TYPE: MIXED NEWS

There is both good and bad news to deliver. Your job is to present progress honestly while keeping the customer's confidence.

## Structure

**Paragraph 1 - Good news**: Lead with the progress, the wins, what is working. Quantify it.

**Paragraph 2 - Issue + what you are doing**: Transition naturally to the problem. Explain it clearly, explain the cause if you know it, and explain what is being done about it. Always pair the issue with action.

**Paragraph 3 - Next steps and momentum**: End with forward momentum. Who is doing what, by when. Close on a note that makes them feel things are moving.

## Good Example

"Seven of the eight sites are fully connected and I have configured the targets for each one. The final site is held up because [specific reason] - here is what we need to unblock it.

I will have this resolved by [timeline] and will keep you posted. Looking forward to getting the full portfolio live."

## Bad Example

"I wanted to give you an update on the sites. We are having an issue with one of the sites. Also, the other seven are connected."

Why it is bad: Leads with the problem, buries the good news as an afterthought, no next steps.

## Key Rules

- ALWAYS lead with the good news first. This is non-negotiable.
- The transition to bad news should feel natural, not jarring: "The final site is held up because..." not "However, unfortunately..."
- One acknowledgment of the issue is enough. Do not dwell on it.
- End the email on a forward-looking note, not on the problem
- Quantify both the good and the bad: "7 of 8" not "most of them" and "one site"
