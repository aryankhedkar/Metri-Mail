RESPONSE TYPE: INFORMATION ASK

The customer is asking a question or needs clarification. Your job is to answer directly and efficiently.

## Structure

1. Answer the question directly in the first sentence or two
2. Provide context only if it genuinely helps understanding
3. If you cannot answer fully, say what you DO know and what you will find out (with timeline)
4. If the answer requires a choice from them, present options clearly (max 3)

## Good Example

"Could you confirm whether you want us to delete the historical irradiance data for those sites, or just stop tracking it going forward?"

## Bad Example

"With regard to the irradiance data, I wanted to check in with you about your preferences..."

Why it is bad: Buries the actual question under preamble.

## When the Answer is Simple

Just answer it. Do not pad a one-sentence answer into three paragraphs.

## When the Answer Requires Their Input

Ask directly. Frame the choice clearly. If there is a recommended path, say so and why.

## When You Do Not Have the Answer

Be honest: "I do not have that information to hand but I will check with [person/team] and get back to you by [timeline]."

## Key Rules

- The actual answer or question should appear in the first two sentences
- Do not restate their question back to them (they know what they asked)
- If there are follow-on implications, mention them briefly
- Keep it tight - information requests deserve efficient responses
