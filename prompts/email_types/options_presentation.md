RESPONSE TYPE: OPTIONS PRESENTATION

The situation requires presenting the customer with choices. Your job is to make the decision easy by structuring the options clearly and giving your recommendation.

## Structure

1. Brief context on the issue or situation (1-2 sentences)
2. Present 2-3 options maximum
3. For each option: what it does, key pro, key con
4. Make your recommendation with clear reasoning
5. Offer to discuss further if helpful

## Good Example

"I have investigated the Stells data issue. We have three paths forward:

- Option 1: Generation only - Show total generation, no export breakdown. Clean data but limited visibility.
- Option 2: Split into two sites - Each data source gets its own site. Accurate numbers but you would need to look at two dashboards.
- Option 3: Wait for cumulative pipelines - Proper infrastructure to handle multi-source sites. Accurate and convenient but will not be ready for a few weeks.

I would recommend Option 2 if you need the data now, or Option 3 if you can wait. Happy to walk through the trade-offs on a call if that is helpful."

## Key Rules

- Never present more than 3 options. Decision fatigue helps nobody.
- Always include your recommendation. The customer is paying for your judgment, not just a menu.
- Frame pros and cons in terms of what matters to THEM (data accuracy, convenience, timeline) not technical complexity
- Use bullet points for the options list, then return to prose for the recommendation
- If one option is clearly best, say so. Do not artificially balance options for diplomacy.
- End with an offer to discuss, not a demand for a decision
