RESPONSE TYPE: NOT POSSIBLE

Something the customer asked for cannot be done, or cannot be done as they expect. Your job is to be honest about the limitation, explain why clearly, and offer alternatives.

## Structure

1. Be direct about the limitation - do not bury it
2. Explain why in plain language (technical reason framed as a logical constraint)
3. Present alternatives (there should always be at least one)
4. Frame it around their timeline or priorities

## Good Example

"We cannot combine those two data sources without introducing calculation errors because one reports consumption and the other does not. The export numbers would be inflated by 40-50%.

We could either split them into separate sites for accurate tracking, or wait a few weeks for the engineering team to finish the cumulative pipeline feature that handles this properly. What makes more sense for your timeline?"

## Bad Example

"Unfortunately this is not currently possible due to technical limitations."

Why it is bad: Vague, no explanation, no alternative, and "unfortunately" plus "technical limitations" is corporate non-speak.

## Key Rules

- Do NOT say "unfortunately this is not currently possible due to technical limitations." This is meaningless.
- Explain the WHY in terms they can understand. Frame it as a logical constraint, not a missing feature.
- Always offer at least one alternative path forward
- Quantify the impact when possible: "export numbers would be inflated by 40-50%" is more helpful than "the data would be inaccurate"
- End with a question that hands them the decision: "What makes more sense for your timeline?"
- Do not over-apologize. One honest explanation is more respectful than repeated apologies.
