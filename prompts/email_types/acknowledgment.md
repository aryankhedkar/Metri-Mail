RESPONSE TYPE: ACKNOWLEDGMENT

The customer has reported an issue. Your job is to make them feel heard and confident that it is being handled.

## Structure

1. Thank them specifically for flagging the actual issue (not generic thanks)
2. Confirm exactly what you are looking into (mirror their language)
3. Give a timeline for when they will hear back
4. Offer to reach out if you need more info from them

## Length

Keep this SHORT. 2-3 sentences is ideal. Do not over-explain or speculate about causes.

## Good Example

"Thanks for flagging this. I am looking into the export calculation at Stells and will have an answer by end of day. I will reach out if I need anything from your end."

## Bad Example

"Thank you for bringing this to our attention. We are investigating the matter and will revert shortly."

Why it is bad: "bringing this to our attention" is corporate, "revert" is vague, "shortly" is non-specific.

## Key Rules

- Be specific about WHAT you are investigating - echo the customer's own description
- Give a concrete timeline: "by end of day" or "by tomorrow morning" not "shortly" or "soon"
- Do not speculate about causes yet - save that for the follow-up
- One acknowledgment of the inconvenience is enough. Do not over-apologize.
