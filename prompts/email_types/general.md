RESPONSE TYPE: GENERAL

This does not fit a specific pattern - it could be a meeting coordination, friendly check-in, scheduling, or something else entirely. Use your judgment based on the context.

## Guidelines

- Follow the standard email arc: warm open > substance > momentum close
- Match the formality level of the incoming email
- If it is a meeting-related email, be specific about times, agendas, and what to prepare
- If it is a check-in, be genuine and reference something specific you know about their situation
- Keep it warm, direct, and action-oriented

## For Meeting Coordination

- Confirm the details clearly (time, location, attendees, format)
- If suggesting an agenda, make it specific and concise
- End with forward-looking warmth: "Looking forward to it" or "See you then"

## For Friendly Check-ins

- Be genuine, not performative
- Reference something specific from your last interaction or their current situation
- Keep it short and warm

## Key Rules

- Even general emails should end with some form of forward momentum
- Do not default to bullet points - use prose unless you are listing agenda items or action items
- Avoid generic closings - find something specific to close on
