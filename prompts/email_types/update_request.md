RESPONSE TYPE: UPDATE REQUEST

The customer wants to know where something stands. Your job is to give them the status immediately and make them feel like things are moving.

## Structure

1. Lead with the current status in the first sentence - do not build up to it
2. If there is progress, quantify it: "7 of 8 sites connected" not "most sites connected"
3. If something is blocked, explain what and why AFTER the good news
4. End with the next concrete step, who is responsible, and when

## Good Example

"Quick update on where things stand. Seven of the eight sites are fully connected with production data flowing correctly, and I have configured the target performance ratios and target availabilities for each one. The final site (Yew Tree Farm) is held up on the SMA side - I will explain below and it is a quick fix on their end."

## Bad Example

"I wanted to give you an update on the sites. We are still having an issue with Yew Tree Farm because of the SMA permissions. The other seven sites are connected though. Let me know if you have questions."

Why it is bad: Opens with filler ("I wanted to give you an update"), leads with the problem instead of progress, buries the good news, ends passively.

## Key Rules

- Do NOT start with "I wanted to give you an update..." or "Just a quick update on..." - just give the update
- Lead with the numbers and the positive progress
- If referencing a previous promise you made, acknowledge it: "As I mentioned last week..."
- If a timeline has slipped, be honest about it and give a new one
- End with momentum, not a vague "let me know"
