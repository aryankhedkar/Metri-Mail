How It Works

Four steps, all automatic

Customer emails come in. MetriMail picks them up, classifies the type, assembles context from our customer database and Knowledge Base, and writes a draft. It lands in Gmail Drafts for review.

Fetch Picks up unread emails, filters out noise
→
Classify 9 types: technical, onboarding, billing, etc.
→
Context Customer data, open issues, KB articles
→
Draft Writes the reply in Gmail Drafts

Knows our customers

Each customer's equipment, contacts, open issues, and promises. Drafts reference the right context every time.

Uses our Knowledge Base

26 articles from supportmetris.vercel.app are loaded in. It references the right troubleshooting or onboarding guide.

Always reviewed

Nothing sends on its own. Anything uncertain gets flagged with [ARYAN: verify] so I catch it before sending.

Live Example

Real email, real draft

Here is what MetriMail produced from a real customer email today.

INBOUND technical_issue
From: Isla Rogers (Ampyrde) · 26 Feb 2026

Good Morning Aryan,

Hope you are well. We are testing the workflow around billing to get ourselves familiar ready for next week when we run the billing cycle. We sent this invoice (to me) as a test, please can you delete this invoice from our account and from the customer portal.

I did not receive an email about the invoice when it was sent, we have not added any customer details to Smeed so it should not have sent it to them. Please can you make sure that this is correct.

Harry is raising other queries in a separate email.

Please let me know when this has been deleted, thanks.

METRIMAIL DRAFT sent after review
To: Isla Rogers · Drafted in 18s

Hi Isla,

Thanks for getting in touch about the test invoice. I will ensure the test invoice is removed from your account and the Customer Portal.

Regarding your concern, I did not see the customer details reflected on Smeed's end; hence, the email not being sent out seems consistent with that setup. I will double-check the system to confirm everything aligns with your current settings.

I will handle the invoice deletion today and confirm once completed. If you notice anything else or have further questions, feel free to let me know. I will follow up with Harry on his separate queries as well.

Looking forward to ensuring everything is seamless for you ahead of the billing cycle.

Best,
Aryan

What it got right

Addressed both asks (delete invoice + confirm Smeed email), reassured on the no-email behaviour, committed to a same-day action, and picked up the Harry thread without being prompted.

Voice rules enforced

No filler opening, no contractions, direct acknowledgment first, clear commitment with timeline, forward-looking close, no em dashes.
