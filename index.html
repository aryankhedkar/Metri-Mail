How It Works

Four steps, all automatic

When a customer emails in, MetriMail picks it up, works out what kind of response it needs (technical issue, update request, onboarding, etc.), grabs the relevant customer context from the database, and writes a draft. It lands in Gmail drafts for me to check and tweak before sending.

Fetch Picks up unread emails from customer domains
→
Classify Works out what kind of reply is needed
→
Context Pulls in who they are, open issues, past promises
→
Draft Writes the reply, drops it in Gmail drafts

Knows our customers

Has a local database with each customer's equipment, contacts, open issues, and promises we have made. Drafts reference the right context.

Sounds like me

Trained on how I write: warm opens, no contractions, good-news-first, no "hope you are well." It follows the same email arc every time.

Always reviewed

Nothing sends on its own. Drafts go to Gmail drafts, and anything the model is not sure about gets flagged with [ARYAN: verify] so I catch it.

Why It Helps

Less writing, same quality

Most of my email time goes into getting the tone right and remembering what we promised each customer. MetriMail handles the first draft so I can focus on getting the content right rather than starting from a blank page.
